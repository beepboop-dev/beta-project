Tips for Making Your QR Menu Work

  1. Place QR codes where people sit. Table tents, stickers on tables, or built into table placards. Don't hide them at the entrance.
  2. Keep a few paper menus for older guests. Not everyone is comfortable with QR codes. Have 3-4 physical menus as backup.
  3. Write descriptions that sell. "Grilled chicken" ‚Üí "Free-range chicken, herb-crusted, with roasted garlic mashed potatoes." Paint a picture.
  4. Use dietary tags religiously. Guests with restrictions will love you for it. It reduces questions to your servers too.
  5. Update seasonally at minimum. A menu that hasn't changed in 6 months feels stale. Even small tweaks keep it fresh.
