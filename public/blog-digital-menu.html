What Is a Digital Menu for Restaurants?

A digital menu is an online version of your restaurant's menu that guests access on their phone. Instead of handing someone a physical menu, you place a QR code on each table. Guests scan it with their phone camera, and your full menu appears ‚Äî beautifully formatted, mobile-friendly, and always up to date.

Key benefit: When you change a price or 86 an item, the update is instant. No reprinting. No crossing things out with a pen. No "sorry, we're out of that."

Why Restaurants Are Switching to Digital Menus

1. Save $2,000+ per year on printing

The average restaurant spends $1,500‚Äì$3,000 per year printing menus. Seasonal changes, price updates, new items ‚Äî every change means a new print run. Digital menus eliminate this cost entirely.

2. Update prices and items in real-time

Food costs fluctuating? Running a weekend special? 86'd the halibut? Update your menu from your phone in 30 seconds. Your guests see the change immediately.

3. Better guest experience

No waiting for a server to bring the menu. No squinting at tiny text in dim lighting. Digital menus are mobile-optimized, searchable, and can include photos that make dishes irresistible.

Guests with allergies or dietary restrictions can instantly filter for vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, or other options. This is a game-changer for inclusive dining.

5. Reduce staff workload

When guests can browse the menu on their phone, servers spend less time explaining dishes and more time delivering great service.
